# YC Startup School — Module 8: Scaling and Sales

Module 8 covers the challenges of scaling beyond early customers. Pete Koomen teaches enterprise sales for technical founders, Tom Blomfield shares a repeatable sales playbook, Nicolas Dessaigne explains how to build a dev tools company, and YC covers how to recruit top engineers and sales reps.

## Key Topics

**Lecture 1 — Enterprise Sales for Founders (Pete Koomen)**
- Why founders must do sales themselves before hiring a sales team
- The enterprise sales cycle: discovery → demo → proof of concept → legal → close
- How to navigate procurement and legal at large companies
- Champion building inside a prospect organization
- Common enterprise sales mistakes by technical founders
- How to price and negotiate enterprise contracts
- When to move from founder-led to team sales

**Lecture 2 — The Sales Playbook for Founders (Tom Blomfield)**
- Building a repeatable sales process from scratch
- Discovery calls: the questions that reveal real pain
- Demo structure: problem → solution → proof → call to action
- Handling objections and follow-up cadence
- CRM basics for early-stage startups
- How to know when your sales process is "ready to hire"
- Metrics for a healthy sales pipeline

**Lecture 3 — How to Start a Dev Tools Company (Nicolas Dessaigne)**
- What makes dev tools businesses unique (bottoms-up adoption, PLG, community)
- How developers discover and adopt new tools
- OSS vs. commercial strategy: when to open-source
- Community-led growth for dev tools
- Developer evangelism and documentation as sales
- Monetization models for dev tools: seats, usage, enterprise tier
- Nicolas's experience building Algolia

**Lecture 4 — How to Recruit the Best Engineers and Sales Reps (Y Combinator)**
- The cost of a bad hire vs. waiting for the right person
- Where to find great engineers: networks, GitHub, referrals, communities
- How to evaluate engineers: what to look for beyond leetcode
- Recruiting sales reps: what signals predict success
- Compensation structures for early engineers (equity-heavy) and sales (commission)
- The hiring process: structured interviews, work tests, reference checks
- How to sell candidates on joining an early-stage startup

### Part 3: Self-Assessment Workbook
Generate 8–10 questions derived from the lectures, for example:
- Are you personally doing every sales call? If you've hired salespeople, what happened?
- Can you describe your current sales process step-by-step? Where do deals die?
- For enterprise deals: who is your champion inside the prospect company?
- How long is your average sales cycle? What is your win rate?
- If building dev tools: do developers discover your product themselves or do they need convincing?
- What is your current recruiting process for engineers? How many offers have you extended vs. accepted?
- Have you run a structured work test before making an engineering hire?

Each question should include context from the specific lecture.
