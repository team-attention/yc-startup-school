## Key Themes from These Founder Stories

### Mark Zuckerberg — Facebook
- **Product preceded the business**: Started as a dorm-room project with no intent to build a company initially
- **Move to where your community is**: Moving to Palo Alto for the summer changed everything — physical location in Silicon Valley opened doors
- **Disciplined expansion**: Launched at Harvard only, then Ivy League, then universities — controlled rollout rather than premature scaling
- **Keep shipping**: Early growth came from constant iteration and adding features users were requesting
- **Founder conviction under pressure**: Turned down acquisition offers including Yahoo's $1B in 2006 because he believed in the mission

### Anne Wojcicki — 23andMe
- **Non-obvious founder background**: Came from finance (hedge fund analyst), not biology — outsider perspective was an asset, not a liability
- **Mission as moat**: Building around democratizing genetic information attracted believers early and sustained the team through adversity
- **Regulatory obstacle as defining moment**: The 2013 FDA warning letter nearly killed the company — navigating it correctly separated 23andMe from competitors
- **B2C → B2B data flywheel**: Started direct-to-consumer, built a massive research database that became the B2B asset
- **Patient as partner**: Patients owning their data and contributing to research created a novel stakeholder model and loyalty flywheel
